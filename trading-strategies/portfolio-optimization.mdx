## Key Optimization Approaches

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Conservative Income" icon="coins">
    Example allocation: 60% bonds, 25% dividend stocks, 15% REITs. Often used to prioritize stability and income.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Balanced Growth" icon="chart-line">
    Example allocation: 40% large-cap, 20% international, 20% bonds, 20% alternatives. Balances growth with diversification.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Aggressive Growth" icon="trending-up">
    Example allocation: 40% growth stocks, 30% tech, 20% emerging markets, 10% alternatives. Designed for higher equity exposure.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Risk Parity" icon="balance-scale">
    Seeks equal risk contribution from each asset class. Often used by hedge funds for diversification.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How Portfolio Optimization Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Asset Selection">
    Choose investments that complement each other — e.g., technology for growth, utilities for stability, international for diversification.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Risk Balancing">
    Distribute capital so each holding contributes appropriately to overall portfolio exposure.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Automatic Rebalancing">
    Maintain allocations through systematic rebalancing based on a chosen schedule or thresholds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Rebalancing Strategies

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Calendar Rebalancing" icon="calendar">
    Rebalance on a predetermined schedule: "Rebalance quarterly back to target allocations."
  </Card>

  <Card title="Threshold Rebalancing" icon="target">
    Rebalance when allocations drift significantly: "Rebalance when any position moves 10% from target."
  </Card>

  <Card title="Tactical Rebalancing" icon="chart-line">
    Adjust based on conditions: "Increase equities during significant market pullbacks."
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
